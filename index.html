LUCY TREPANIER'S

BINDING SITE

Introduction

↑

Who we are online isn't who we are offline. The moment we step onto a screen, something shifts.

This project traces that shift. Drawing on Paul Ford, Pérez Villoro & Hamamoto, and Frank Chimero, I'm looking at the hidden architecture of technology. The invisible rules that shape how we show up, what we reveal, and what gets designed out of us.

to these ideas directly. Grids borrowed from code structure, color palettes that move between states like identity does, animations that mimic surveillance, and screen behavior. Typography, texture, and motion do the work of guiding you through the readings, making the connections visible and felt.

Dark mode, cool tones, bold sans serifs. The layout prioritizes breathing room and subtle motion. Textures throughout feature artwork by Art Director Bárbara Abbês, originally published in AIGA Eye on Design.

Created and developed as a study of digital identity, responsiveness, and how we move through screens.
